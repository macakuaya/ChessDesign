# Train

## Openings

### Playing the Opening

Opening Practice lets you train practical opening play against Coach. Coach will play moves that you are likely to experience in-game for a user of your rating. This is done by referencing an "All Chesscom Game Explorer" (ie, all games every played on Chesscom, filtered by rating band)

Users can select a starting position by color/opening family (eg, Caro Kann) or go thru the Advanced route to input a specific FEN or set up the board or make moves until they reach the position they want to start from. The Personal Training Plan will recommend a starting position for you.

Users will have to play 10 moves past the position they start from. They will be graded on the Accuracy of these 10 moves and higher Accuracy will result in more Train points.

### Your Opening Mistakes

Retry positions where you left book by playing a bad move. Play any good move. Bonus points for playing a book move.

A session will involve 5-10 move retries. Train points will be allocated based on number of good retries.

## Tactics

### Your Tactical Misses

Retry mistakes you made in your actual games. Misses are when you had the opportunity to win material/find mate but didn't. There will be rules to define a satisfying miss to retry. We will want to know, for a given ruleset, the number of misses a user will generate per game.

A session will involve 5-10 move retries. Train points will be allocated based on number of correct retries.

### Blunder or Brilliant

Take puzzles from our Puzzles DB, making sure that the pitch move was a blunder. Show the user the position pre-pitch move. Make them choose between the best move in the position and the pitch move (blunder) from the puzzle. Users and puzzles should have a specific rating for this exercise so we can serve content at the correct difficulty level.

A session will involve 5-10 puzzles. Train points will be allocated based on number of correct moves.

### Themed Puzzles

Let users choose puzzles to do by theme (eg, fork). Serve puzzles to them at their level. Indicate the user's skill level at each tactic (per their Adv Stats) when they go to choose the puzzles they wish to practice. We should also allow users to choose puzzles by Opening.

A session will involve 5-10 puzzles. Train points will be allocated based on number of correct puzzles.

## Strategy

### Your Strategic Mistakes

### Attacking

Identify Attacking positions from the user's game where they should have converted but ended up giving away the advantage (if they went from winning to equal/losing but ended up winning later on, that still counts as throwing it away). Include metadata such as position type (open, semi-open, closed) so the user can select a type of position to practice.

Users will get served these positions to play out against Coach. The goal will be to turn their attacking advantage into something concrete—mate or up a large amount of material (piece).

Assess users on their accuracy of play in the game, giving them more Train points for higher accuracy.

### Defending

Identify Defending positions from the user's game where they could have held but ended up losing. Users will get served these positions to play out against Coach. The goal will be to turn their defensive position into something more statically equal—survive some number of moves and reach a quiet position.

Assess users on their accuracy of play in the game, giving them more Train points for higher accuracy.

### Strategic Puzzles

Users will be served puzzles where they have to accomplish a strategic goal. These include improving their piece activity/pawn structure, or harming their opponent's. Users and puzzles should have a specific rating for this exercise so we can serve content at the correct difficulty level.

A session will involve 5-10 move retries. Train points will be allocated based on number of correct puzzles.

## Endgames

### Convert the Win

Identify Endgame positions that the user should have won, but ended up losing/drawing. Include metadata such as endgame theme (eg, King and Pawn, Rook and Pawn, etc) so users can drill specific types of endgames.

Users will get served these positions to play out against Coach. They will either have to win by checkmate or safely promote a pawn or maybe be safely up a piece.

Assess users on their accuracy of play in the game, giving them more Train points for higher accuracy.

### Hold the Draw

Identify Endgame positions that the user should have held, but ended up losing. Include metadata such as endgame theme (eg, King and Pawn, Rook and Pawn, etc) so users can drill specific types of endgames.

Users will get served these positions to play out against Coach. They will have to hold a draw through the end of the game.

Assess users on their accuracy of play in the game, giving them more Train points for higher accuracy.
